Bridging research, teaching, and public engagement

My work focuses on using Type Ia supernovae as standardisable candles to map cosmic expansion and probe the nature of dark energy. I also tutor physics and maths at GCSE, A-Level, and undergraduate level, and am actively engaged in public outreach and science communication.

The expansion of the universe is accelerating, but why?

By studying brilliant stellar explosions called Type Ia supernovae, astronomers discovered that the universe isn't just expanding - it's speeding up. Something mysterious, known as dark energy, is driving this acceleration. My research focuses on refining measurements of these supernovae, and finding more of them, to more precisely probe the nature of this unseen force.

Understanding the universe starts here!

Alongside my research, I teach physics and maths to students from GCSE and A-level up to university level. I focus on building real understanding - not just memorising formulae - so that every problem connects back to how the universe works. Whether you're preparing for exams or exploring beyond the syllabus, I aim to make physics and maths clear, confident, and enjoyable.

Sharing the wonders of the cosmos!

I give talks and workshops on astronomy and cosmology for schools, science festivals, and the public. From exploding stars to the fate of the universe, I love helping people see how modern physics connects to our everyday curiosity about the night sky. If you'd like a speaker for your event or classroom, I'd be happy to help inspire the next generation of scientists.
