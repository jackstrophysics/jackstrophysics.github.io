Hi, I'm Jack!

I'm a 2nd year DPhil / PhD student at the University of Oxford, supervised by Professors Stephen Smartt and Maria Vincenzi. My research explores how Type Ia supernovae can be used to chart the expansion history of the Universe, and reveal the nature of the mysterious force known as dark energy. I'm building a new supernova sample in the local universe from the ATLAS survey, and developing models that connect these explosions to the galaxies that host them.


Before coming to Oxford, I completed an MPhys in Physics with Astrophysics at the University of Bath, graduating with first-class honours and receiving the Chancellor's and Ayliffe Prizes for academic excellence.


Alongside my research, I tutor physics and mathematics to students from GCSE to undergraduate level, and I'm actively involved in public outreach and science communication. I love sharing the wonders of the cosmos with others, whether through one-on-one tutoring or engaging talks and workshops.

The expansion of the universe is accelerating, but why?

By studying brilliant stellar explosions called Type Ia supernovae, astronomers discovered that the universe isn't just expanding - it's speeding up. Something mysterious, known as dark energy, is driving this acceleration. My research focuses on refining measurements of these supernovae, and finding more of them, to more precisely probe the nature of this unseen force.

Understanding the universe starts here!

Alongside my research, I teach physics and maths to students from GCSE and A-level up to university level. I focus on building real understanding - not just memorising formulae - so that every problem connects back to how the universe works. Whether you're preparing for exams or exploring beyond the syllabus, I aim to make physics and maths clear, confident, and enjoyable.

Sharing the wonders of the cosmos!

I give talks and workshops on astronomy and cosmology for schools, science festivals, and the public. From exploding stars to the fate of the universe, I love helping people see how modern physics connects to our everyday curiosity about the night sky. If you'd like a speaker for your event or classroom, I'd be happy to help inspire the next generation of scientists.

Bridging research, teaching, and public engagement

  • Type Ia Supernova Cosmology

    Constraining dark energy by building a new low-redshift supernova sample from the ATLAS survey to anchor future cosmological work with LSST.

  • Data Analysis & Modelling

    Developing Python-based pipelines for lightcurve fitting, simulation, and Bayesian inference, using tools such as astropy, sncosmo, and emcee.

  • Teaching & Mentoring

    Tutor and lab demonstrator for astrophysics at Oxford, and private physics and mathematics tutor with over 400 hours of experience and 5★ feedback.

  • Public Engagement

    Speaker for Oxford and national outreach programmes, including UNIQ and the Oxford Physics Summer Schools — inspiring students with talks like “Flashes in the Abyss.”

  • Scientific Computing

    Experience in Python, C, and high-performance computing for astrophysical simulations - from radiative transfer and Monte Carlo methods to supernova population modelling.

  • Awards & Recognition

    Recipient of the University of Bath Chancellor's and Ayliffe Prizes, STFC DPhil Studentship, and multiple Oxford research grants for conference participation.
